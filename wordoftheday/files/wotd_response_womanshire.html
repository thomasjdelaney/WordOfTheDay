      What does the noun womanshire mean?

      There is one meaning in OED's entry for the noun womanshire. See ‘Meaning & use’ for definition, usage, and quotation evidence.

      This word is now obsolete. It is only recorded in the early 1600s.

      See meaning & use

      Where does the noun womanshire come from?

      Earliest known use

      early 1600s

      The only known use of the noun womanshire is in the early 1600s.

      OED's only evidence for womanshire is from 1606, in Wily Beguilde.

      womanshire is formed within English, by compounding.

      Etymons: woman n., shire n.
