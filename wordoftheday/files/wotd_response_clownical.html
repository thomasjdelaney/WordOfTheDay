      Factsheet

      What does the adjective clownical mean?

      There is one meaning in OED's entry for the adjective clownical. See ‘Meaning & use’ for definition, usage, and quotation evidence.

      This word is now obsolete. It is last recorded around the 1870s.

      See meaning & use

      Where does the adjective clownical come from?

      Earliest known use

      early 1600s

      The earliest known use of the adjective clownical is in the early 1600s.

      OED's earliest evidence for clownical is from 1614, in the writing of John Cooke, judge and regicide.

      clownical is formed within English, by derivation.

      Etymons: clown n., ‑ic suffix, ‑al suffix1.

      See etymology

      Etymology

      Summary

      Formed within English, by derivation.
      Etymons: clown n., ‑ic suffix, ‑al suffix1.
      < clown n. + ‑ic suffix + ‑al suffix1, probably after comical adj.

      Compare farcical adj.1

      Compare earlier clownish adj., clown-like adj.
