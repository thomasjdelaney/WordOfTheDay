      Where does the word nil admirari come from?

      Earliest known use

      mid 1600s

      The earliest known use of the word nil admirari is in the mid 1600s.

      OED's earliest evidence for nil admirari is from 1654, in the writing of Henry Hammond, Church of England clergyman and theologian.

      nil admirari is a borrowing from Latin.

      Etymology

      Summary

      A borrowing from Latin.
      Etymon: Latin nīl admīrārī.
      < classical Latin nīl admīrārī, in nil admirari prope res est una..quae possit facere et servare beatum, ‘to wonder at nothing is just about the only way a man can become contented and remain so’…   
      < classical Latin nīl admīrārī, in nil admirari prope res est una..quae possit facere et servare beatum, ‘to wonder at nothing is just about the only way a man can become contented and remain so’ (Horace Epistles 1. 6. 1) < nīl nil n.1 + admīrārī admire v.

      1. noun
        1. 1654–
          The attitude of indifference to the distractions of the outside world advocated by the Roman poet Horace. Also: a person adopting this attitude.
          Occasionally used irregularly as infinitive phrase: to admire nothing.
          1. 1654
            I am put under discipline to teach me..the Nil Admirari, to see, and hear the most portentous things, and to admire at nothing.
            H. Hammond, Answer to Animadversions on Dissertations touching Ignatius's Epistles iv. §2 98
          2. 1741
            Believe me, I have often try'd To take your fav'rite Maxim for my Guide. Nil admirari dwells upon your Tongue.
            S. Duck, Every Man in his Own Way 2
          3. 1748
            This book..will both divert and astonish you; and, at the same time, teach you nil admirari.
            Lord Chesterfield, Letter 27 September (1932) (modernized text) vol. IV. 1233
          4. 1785
            But Dr. Johnson has much of the nil admirari in smaller concerns.
            J. Boswell, Journal of Tour to Hebrides 26 August 118
          5. 1821
            I ne'er could see the very Great happiness of the ‘Nil Admirari’.
            Lord Byron, Don Juan: Canto V c. 185
          6. 1822
            The nil admirari..seems to me a bad sign in a young person.
            P. B. Shelley, Letter 18 June (1964) vol. II. 436
          7. 1876
            The critic-coolness in their eyes Disclaims emotion's shallow sea; Or misapply they precept wise, Nil admirari?
            H. Melville, Clarel vol. I. i. xli. 143
          8. 1951
            Nil admirari was his precept, the salute to genius his practice.
            N. Annan, Leslie Stephen x. 278
          9. 1961
            Why was I always preaching nil admirari?
            M. Kelly, Spoilt Kill iii. 193
          10. 1990
            Nil admirari is characteristic of Cambridge, and I enjoy Cannadine's cool tone.
            New York Review of Books 6 December 30/2

      2. adjective
        1. 1821–
          Expressing or adopting an attitude or philosophy of indifference to the outside world.
          1. 1821
            I pity..the nil admirari apathy which can view such a scene with indifference.
            Literary Speculum November 45
          2. 1857
            Very many men now-a-days..adopt or affect to adopt the nil admirari doctrine.
            A. Trollope, Barchester Towers vol. III. xvi. 277
          3. a1865
            Every inflexion of the voice breathed out..admiration! And this from the nil admirari brother.
            E. C. Gaskell, Wives & Daughters (1866) vol. I. xxi. 237
          4. 1935
            Mr. Glynde's nil admirari countenance..registered surprise.
            J. Buchan, House of Four Winds vi. 139
          5. 1992
            Those Epicurean shrugs of the shoulder and nil admirari smiles.
            Times (Nexis) 31 March
